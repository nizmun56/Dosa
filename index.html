What is Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is typically served for breakfast or as a snack. It is a pancake made from rice flour and ground pulses, and is usually served with a spiced vegetable filling1. The dish is famous for its simple ingredients and savory, slightly bitter flavor2. The crispy and crepe-like texture of dosa makes it a popular street food in India. It can be enjoyed with various fillings and sauces, such as potato, cheese, and chutneys2. To make dosa, a mixture of rice flour and black gram flour is used as the base of the batter. The batter is then fermented and spread thinly on a hot griddle or tava. Once cooked, the dosa is served hot and crispy2.

How does it taste

What does dosa taste like exactly? As described, it's a thin, crisp crepe or pancake. It has a slight tangy taste due to the fermentation, and is savory rather than sweet. Without the filling, on its own it's called 'plain dosa' that can be eaten with a variety of vegetable curries

What to eat with it

Traditionally, dosa is served with a bowl of sambar and coconut chutney on the side. Some darshinis also serve a spicy tomato chutney along with the other accompaniments.

How to eat Dosa

Since Dosa is typically a street food, it's easy to take and eat on the go. In fact, it's customary in the Indian culture to eat Dosa with your hands! I like to tear it off and eat it in small bites. ... If it's served with chutney, you can use a knife to spread some over your Dosa and eat it bit by bit.

Why I like it

Dosa is famous for its simple ingredients, interesting flavors, and even its sweet aroma. I love it for how easy and clean the ingredients are.
